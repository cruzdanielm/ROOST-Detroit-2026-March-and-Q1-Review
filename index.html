Executive Summary

A breakout quarter — volume outperformed plan, flow-through delivered record EBITDA.

March delivered $650K of Rooms Revenue, 15% ahead of budget and 24% above prior year, at 71% occupancy against a 58% plan. Gross Operating Profit of $245K came in $93K ahead of budget (+61%) and EBITDA of $182K beat plan by $86K (+89%). Quarter-to-date, the asset has delivered $330K of EBITDA — $171K (+107%) ahead of budget and $75K (+29%) ahead of prior year — on a dollar-flat expense base relative to plan. The story is clear: demand arrived in excess of the rate plan, and the operating model converted it.

The one-line read

Occupancy is doing the work, extended-stay has fully returned, and the cost base is holding flat to plan — three forces that compounded into an exceptional Q1 EBITDA print. March ran 13 points ahead of plan on occupancy, Q1 ran ~8 points ahead. ADR sat $19 below budget in March and $20 below Q1 YTD, so the top-line beat is entirely volume-driven. Extended-stay (30+ night) revenue has more than doubled YoY, reversing last year's roll-off. The watch items heading into Q2 are (1) re-opening the ADR gap on compression nights, (2) sustaining the extended-stay book without over-indexing to it, and (3) holding the margin gain that emerged in March.

What drove the quarter

Performance highlights

  • Volume outperformance. Rooms sold 6,454 YTD vs 5,630 plan (+824, +14.6%) and 5,515 LY (+939, +17.0%). March alone ran 2,611 RN vs 2,116 plan.
  • Extended-stay recovery. 30+ night revenue doubled to $407K YTD (26% of book) from $189K a year ago — driven by Rocket / Bedrock Extended Stay, Henry Ford Health, and Rocket Mortgage.
  • Expense discipline. Undistributed operating expenses $723K YTD vs $811K budget — $89K under plan on Admin/G&A (-$18K), Sales & Marketing (-$21K), Property Operations (-$22K), Info&Telecom (-$8K), and Utilities (-$20K).
  • Q1 GOP margin expansion. 31.9% vs 21.1% budget — over 10 points of margin leverage from revenue volume meeting structurally-fixed costs.

Strategic areas of focus

Three items to manage into Q2

Rate positioning. ADR of $239 ran $19 behind March budget and $11 behind PY; Q1 ADR of $240 ran $20 behind budget. The book traded rate for share — recapturing rate on high-compression nights in Q2 is the highest-value single lever.

Extended-stay sustainability. The 30+ night segment grew +$218K YoY, concentrated in a handful of accounts (Rocket / Bedrock conglomerate ~$335K consolidated, Henry Ford Health). Diversifying the long-stay book protects against a single-customer step-down.

Margin protection. The Q1 margin gain rests on dollar-flat opex. Holding utilities, payroll, and commissions through peak-season volume is the operational priority.

What moved the month

Three drivers behind exceeding EBITDA by $86K vs budget

What it means

Revenue & Expense Drivers

What actually moved March & Q1
Demand · Rate · Mix · Cost
Drivers · March & Q1 YTD

Volume on the top line; discipline on the P&L.

The Q1 revenue beat is entirely volume-driven — Rooms Sold +14.6% vs budget offset an ADR gap of ~$20. On the cost side, every major Undistributed Expense category finished under budget, producing $89K of expense savings on a volume base 14.6% higher than plan — the textbook signature of operating leverage.

Revenue drivers

Where the top-line beat originated

  • March occupancy surprise. 71.4% actual vs 57.8% budget; room nights sold +23.4% vs plan.
  • Extended-stay return. 30+ night revenue +$218K YoY — Rocket/Bedrock ($314K combined), Henry Ford Health (+$33K), and 38 net-new accounts driving the lift.
  • Other Rooms Revenue uplift. Ancillary revenue of $56K YTD (+$12K vs plan, +$12K vs PY) on fees, pet, and parking attachment.
  • Distribution expansion. AMEXGBT production up to $31K YTD from $2K PY — managed-corporate-travel channel producing at scale.
  • Direct-channel base holding. ROOST Detroit Direct Sales steady at $34K — incremental to the Bedrock institutional book.
  • Events / cultural pickup. Detroit Opera House +$21K YoY on performance-season blocks.

Expense drivers

What held the cost base below plan

  • Utilities discipline. $138K YTD vs $158K budget (-$20K, -12.7%). Energy management is outperforming plan.
  • Property Operations & Maintenance. $191K vs $213K (-$22K, -10.3%). Preventive-maintenance cadence is holding reactive work down.
  • Sales & Marketing. $170K vs $191K (-$21K, -10.9%). Group and extended-stay demand arrived with lower-than-planned advertising spend.
  • Administrative & G&A. $165K vs $183K (-$18K, -9.9%).
  • Info & Telecom Systems. $59K vs $67K (-$8K, -11.7%).
  • Rooms labor efficiency. Departmental salaries & wages $168K YTD vs $179K plan despite +15% volume — no proportional step-up required.
  • Offsetting pressure. Property Taxes +$10K (+13.4% vs budget) and Management Fees +$3K on higher revenue base — both natural variable escalation.
Bottom line

Volume met a structurally-fixed cost base, and the margin showed up. The Q1 outperformance is genuinely flow-through — incremental room nights arrived at ~95% contribution margin because the staffing, marketing, and facilities cost base did not have to flex proportionally. The counterbalance is symmetrical: if Q2 volume softens, Utilities, Property Operations, and Management Fees do not flex down at the same pace. Sustaining occupancy through peak season is what converts the Q1 print into the 2026 run-rate.

Risks & Opportunities · Q2 Outlook

Four risks to manage and four opportunities to monetize.

The asset is tracking well ahead of plan. The items that could compress the Q1 flow-through are (a) the ADR gap to plan, (b) concentration in the Rocket/Bedrock ecosystem, (c) guest-survey distribution volume, and (d) property-tax pressure. Peak-season demand signals support pricing into compression and broadening the corporate book.

Risk · ADR Gap to Plan

ADR of $239 Q1 ran $20 below budget and $11 below PY. Every $5 of ADR recaptured equals ~$125K annualized at current occupancy. Peak-season compression nights are the window.

Risk · Rocket / Bedrock Concentration

Rocket / Bedrock conglomerate $335K YTD consolidated (~21% of Rooms Revenue) across nine booking entities. A healthy, growing partnership — but structural concentration. Diversifying the long-stay book reduces single-counterparty exposure.

Risk · Survey & Review Distribution

Emails sent -21% YoY; TA submissions -48%. Public-review volume supports conversion. Restoring post-stay trigger coverage is the Q2 priority.

Risk · Property Tax & Management Fees

Property taxes +$10K (+13%) vs budget YTD; management fees +$3K. Both variable-escalation categories — modest drag on flow-through as revenue rises.

Opp · Rate Recapture on Peak Nights

Close the $20 ADR gap on high-compression dates — Tigers home stands, event weekends, citywide conferences. Discipline on compression is the highest-value Q2 move.

Opp · Extended-Stay Diversification

Build three to five net-new 30+ night accounts outside the Rocket ecosystem. Healthcare (Henry Ford, DMC), education, and film/production are priority verticals.

Opp · Corporate-Travel Channel Scale

AMEXGBT $31K YTD from $2K — Concur, TRX, and TMC integrations can scale this meaningfully. Penetrating Ford and GM managed-travel programs sits on top.

Opp · Guest-Experience Protection

Detractor share dropped to 5% and rating improved +6%. Protect the lead through peak season; lift TA review volume via widget placement in confirmation/post-stay flows.

Strategic summary

Q1 outperformance is real and built on durable pillars — volume, mix, and expense discipline. Protecting it requires pricing into peak-season compression, diversifying the extended-stay book beyond the Rocket ecosystem, and holding the Undistributed expense run-rate as volume continues to rise. The upside case is straightforward: close 30–50% of the ADR gap on compression dates and Q2 EBITDA scales proportionally.
